Ancient History (3000 BCE - 500 CE)

Foundation of human civilization and early empires

3300-1300 BCE

Indus Valley Civilization

One of the world's earliest urban civilizations. Major cities: Harappa, Mohenjo-daro. Known for advanced drainage systems and urban planning.

Harappa Mohenjo-daro Urban Planning
1500-500 BCE

Vedic Period

Composition of Vedas, establishment of caste system, and development of early Hindu philosophy. Transition from nomadic to settled agriculture.

Rigveda Caste System Agriculture
326 BCE

Alexander's Invasion

Alexander the Great invaded India, fought Battle of Hydaspes against King Porus. Marked the beginning of Indo-Greek cultural exchange.

Battle of Hydaspes King Porus Hellenistic Influence
322-185 BCE

Mauryan Empire

Founded by Chandragupta Maurya, reached zenith under Ashoka. First pan-Indian empire. Ashoka's edicts spread Buddhism.

Chandragupta Maurya Ashoka Buddhism
320-550 CE

Gupta Empire

Golden Age of Indian culture, science, and mathematics. Achievements in astronomy, medicine, and arts. Decimal system developed.

Golden Age Aryabhata Decimal System

Major Wars & Battles

Battle of Hydaspes (326 BCE)

Alexander vs King Porus. Despite defeat, Porus's valor impressed Alexander.

Kalinga War (261 BCE)

Ashoka's conquest of Kalinga. The bloodshed led to Ashoka's conversion to Buddhism.

Medieval History (500-1500 CE)

Rise of regional kingdoms and Islamic invasions

711 CE

Arab Conquest of Sindh

Muhammad bin Qasim conquered Sindh. First Islamic invasion of Indian subcontinent. Introduced Islamic culture and administrative practices.

Muhammad bin Qasim Islamic Invasion Cultural Exchange
1001-1027 CE

Mahmud of Ghazni's Invasions

17 invasions of India, primarily for wealth. Destroyed many temples including Somnath. Established Ghazni as a major power.

17 Invasions Somnath Temple Wealth Plunder
1192 CE

Second Battle of Tarain

Muhammad Ghori defeated Prithviraj Chauhan. Marked the beginning of Muslim rule in North India.

Muhammad Ghori Prithviraj Chauhan Muslim Rule
1206-1526 CE

Delhi Sultanate

Five dynasties ruled: Slave, Khilji, Tughlaq, Sayyid, Lodi. Established Islamic administrative system in India.

Five Dynasties Qutub Minar Islamic Administration
1336-1672 CE

Vijayanagara Empire

Hindu empire in South India. Patron of arts and literature. Hampi was the capital. Resisted Muslim expansion in the south.

Hampi Hindu Empire Arts & Literature

Major Wars & Battles

Battle of Tarain (1191-1192)

First battle won by Prithviraj, second by Muhammad Ghori. Turning point in Indian history.

Battle of Panipat I (1526)

Babur defeated Ibrahim Lodi. Established Mughal rule in India.

Battle of Talikota (1565)

Deccan Sultanates defeated Vijayanagara Empire. End of Hindu resistance in South.

Modern History (1500-1947 CE)

Colonial period and independence struggle

1526-1857 CE

Mughal Empire

Founded by Babur, reached zenith under Akbar and Aurangzeb. Declined after Aurangzeb's death. Cultural synthesis of Hindu-Muslim traditions.

Babur Akbar Taj Mahal
1498 CE

Vasco da Gama's Arrival

Portuguese explorer reached Calicut. Beginning of European colonization. Established sea route to India.

Portuguese Calicut Sea Route
1600 CE

East India Company

British East India Company established. Initially for trade, gradually gained political control. Battle of Plassey (1757) marked beginning of British rule.

Trade Battle of Plassey British Rule
1857 CE

Sepoy Mutiny

First War of Independence. Triggered by cartridge issue. Leaders: Mangal Pandey, Rani Laxmibai, Tatya Tope. Marked end of EIC rule.

Mangal Pandey Rani Laxmibai Independence War
1885-1947 CE

Freedom Struggle

Indian National Congress formed (1885). Gandhi's non-violence movement. Quit India Movement (1942). Independence achieved on August 15, 1947.

Gandhi Non-violence Independence

Major Wars & Battles

Battle of Plassey (1757)

Robert Clive defeated Siraj-ud-Daulah. Established British supremacy in Bengal.

Anglo-Mysore Wars (1767-1799)

Four wars between British and Mysore. Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan resisted British expansion.

Anglo-Maratha Wars (1775-1818)

Three wars that ended Maratha power. Established British supremacy in India.
